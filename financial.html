Financial Analysis

Financial Aspects

Revenue & Sales

Microsoft has consistently demonstrated impressive revenue growth across its key segments. Over the last few years, its annual revenue has been growing at an average rate of approximately 10-15%. Its cloud computing segment, Azure, has experienced even more substantial growth, with revenue increasing by around 40%-50% year-over-year. This growth trajectory underscores Microsoft's position as a leading player in the cloud service market.

Profitability

Microsoft's focus on high-margin products and services, coupled with efficent cost management, has led to significant profitability. In recent years, the company has achieved an average annual growth in profitability of approximately 15%-20%. This growth is particularly noteworthy given Microsoft's strategic shift towards subscription-based models, which often yield recurring revenue streams and higher margins over time.

Debt levels

Despite its growth, Microsoft has maintained prudent debt management practices, ensuring its financial health remain strong. While specific percentage growth figures for debt levels may vary, Microsoft's debt-to-equity ratio has remained stable or even decreased slightly over time. This indicates the company's ability to finance its growth initiatives without overly relying on debt, thus mitigating financial risk for investors.

Earning Per Share

Microsoft's earning per share(EPS) growth averaged 17.50% in the past decade and accelerating to 22.00% over the last five years. This consistant growth reflects Microsoft's strong performances across its diverse portfolio. Compared to its competitors, Microsoft's EPS growth stands out due to its sustained momentum, driven by its market leadership, innovation, and ability to capture opportunities in rapidly evolving technology markets.

Return on Asset / Return on Equity

Return on asset

Microsoft's return on assets measures it's efficency in generating profit relative to its total assets.Over the recent years, Microsoft's ROA have been great, averaging around 12-15%. In there most recent report, the company has an ROA of 19.8%, which reflects strong operational performance and strategic investments to generate profit.

Return on Equity

Microsoft has consistently maintained a high return on equity(ROE) over the recent quaters. In the latest quater ending December 31, 2023, Microsoft achieved an ROE of 38.40%, continuing its trend of strong profitability and value creation for shareholders. This trend is consistent with previous quaters, where Microsoft's ROE ranged between 38.20% and 39.32%. Compared to previous year Micosoft have shown great stability, with an average ROE exceeding 40% in 2021.

Liquidity

Microsoft has a current ratio of 1.22, which indicates good short-term financial strength. This current ratio helps measure Microsoft's ability to pay its short-term liabilites with its short-term assets. A current ratio above 1 indicates that microsoft has more current assets than current liabilities, which suggests good liquidity.

Comparative Analysis

  • Strong Cash Position

    Microsoft maintains a great cash reserve, providing flexibilty for investments and acquisitions. Microsoft has $80.98 Billion as cash on hand. In fact, Apple, Google, and Microsoft make up for 92% of cash reserves that Yahoo Finance could document.

  • Diverse Revenue Stream

    Microsoft operates across various segnments which icnludes business products like Office 365 and LinkedIn, cloud services such as Azure, and comsumer offerings like Xbox gaming and Surface devices. This diversification helps Microsoft maintain stability and competitiveness in the tech industry by tapping into many markets, providing solutions to consumer needs.

  • Dividend payout

    Microsoft's dividend payment stands out due to its consistency and growth over the years. Microsoft has a yield around 0.71% which may seem low but what makes it attractive is its history of consistent increases. Microsoft has been increasing its dividend annually for over a decade, with an average annual growth rate of around 10%. Apple dividend yield is around 0.56% while Google doesn't pay dividend at all, they also lack consistent dividend growth that Microsoft provides.
